## Look-Ahead Audit

### Check: Outcome Before Signal Timestamp

**Result: NEGATIVE — no temporal anomalies found.**

All signal records files are post-hoc (written after the bar closes). The `timestamp` field is the signal generation time, and `outcome` reflects what happened during the bar. In all 74 examined records:

- `outcome` is always `hit_sl`, `hit_tp`, or `rejected_*`
- No outcome precedes its signal's `timestamp`
- `tick_evidence.timestamp` always equals or closely follows the signal timestamp

### Check: Same-Bar SL/TP Ambiguity

**Result: NO AMBIGUITY OBSERVED, but the ambiguity rule is documentable.**

For SELL signals (dominant direction in records):
- SL price is _above_ entry (geometrically correct for SELL stop-loss)
- TP price is _below_ entry (geometrically correct for SELL take-profit)

For BUY signals:
- SL price is _below_ entry
- TP price is _above_ entry

**Ambiguity rule used by the system:** The shadow runner resolves per-bar SL vs TP outcomes by checking SL _before_ TP. If price crosses both levels within the same bar (theoretically possible but not observed in this data), SL is credited. This is a conservative rule. In all 74 records examined, only one level was triggered per signal (either SL or TP, never both), so the rule was never exercised.
